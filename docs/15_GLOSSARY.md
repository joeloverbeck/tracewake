# Glossary

## Actor

An entity capable of perception, belief, intention, and action. Usually a person, but institutions may act through roles and procedures.

## Agent

The decision-making system associated with an actor.

## Belief

A subjective proposition held by an agent, with confidence and provenance. Belief may be true, false, stale, contradictory, or speculative.

## Causal graph

A graph linking events to causes, enabling conditions, intentions, observations, traces, and consequences.

## Contract

A formal obligation between parties, usually with verification and sanctions.

## Event

An immutable record of a meaningful occurrence, failed attempt, observation, report, or state change.

## Event sourcing

An architecture where state changes are stored as a sequence of events and current state is derived from replay/projection.

## Ground truth

The authoritative world state and event history. Agents do not automatically know it.

## Institution

A social system with roles, records, norms, procedures, resources, and jurisdiction. Examples: household, town authority, guild, temple, market.

## Intention

A committed goal or plan an agent is currently pursuing.

## Lead

A player-facing clue, artifact, rumor, report, or opportunity. Unlike a quest, a lead does not imply truth, solvability, or guaranteed reward.

## Norm

A rule with social/institutional meaning: obligation, prohibition, permission, status rule, or constitutive rule.

## Notice

A physical or public information artifact, usually produced by an institution or actor, communicating structured claims.

## Possession

The player’s controller binding to an actor. Possession should not alter world truth or actor knowledge by default.

## Projection

A derived view of event history or state, such as current inventory, belief store, public ledger, UI card, or case file.

## Quest

A conventional game concept intentionally avoided as a primary engine type. In this project, quest-like experiences emerge as UI projections over incidents, requests, contracts, obligations, and opportunities.

## Routine

A recurring intention pattern, such as workday or mealtime, that remains conditional and interruptible.

## Salience

A measure that helps UI surface interesting events or chains. Salience may highlight but must not cause events.

## Stale information

Information that may once have been accurate but no longer matches ground truth.

## Trace

A world artifact or mental/social/institutional residue left by an event. Traces can be observed, interpreted, altered, erased, or misunderstood.

## Violation

An action that breaks a norm. A violation can occur without being detected or sanctioned.
