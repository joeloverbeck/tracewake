# First Proof: Missing Expected Property

## Identity

The first proof is **Missing Expected Property**, with **The Missing Property Village** as the canonical first fixture.

This name is intentionally plain. The proof may use Tomas, Mara, Elena, Anna, Elias, the reeve, a strongbox, and coins, but it must not overfit to that object set. The proof is not the notice lifecycle. It is not a road-threat bounty. It is not a companion expedition. It is the ordinary-life causal chain that makes a missing object matter.

```text
property expected somewhere
 -> property moved or taken for modeled reasons
 -> absence discovered
 -> partial knowledge propagates
 -> record created from claims
 -> wrong suspicion arises
 -> possession parity proves no knowledge leak
 -> debug explains truth, belief, traces, records, and consequences
```

If a village cannot do this with an expected object, container, place, actor, or custody relation, it has not earned road threats, notice boards, companions, or regional travel.

## Target scale

Start with roughly 10–20 high-detail named agents.

Expansion target after stability: 10–30 high-detail agents.

Optional low-LOD background population is allowed only if it is causally honest and promotable. Low-LOD people must have identity, household/role context, summary needs, possessions/custody, salient beliefs, and ancestry. They may not be crowd props.

Do not target 30–60 varied-LOD agents for the first proof. That scale is too large before the village can explain a stolen or moved item.

## First domain

The first domain is neutral medieval-ish ordinary life without magic.

Use it because it makes the first proof legible:

- homes;
- rooms;
- doors;
- containers;
- storage;
- beds;
- food;
- work;
- local authority;
- slow information;
- testimony;
- gossip;
- records;
- household privacy;
- property expectations;
- simple money/custody.

The kernel must not become medieval, fantasy, feudal, combat-centric, monster-aware, or magic-aware. Domain flavor is content. Causal contracts are kernel architecture.

## Actor roster

### Required named actors

Tomas:
: miller, property owner, likely victim. He expects coin_stack_01 or equivalent property in strongbox_tomas. He has work obligations and household concerns.

Mara:
: desperate worker, debtor, thief or property mover. She has hunger/debt/work pressure, partial knowledge that Tomas stores money, and enough motive/opportunity to attempt theft or risky movement if conditions align.

Elena:
: uncertain witness. She may hear, glimpse, misremember, or misinterpret something. She must not become a perfect clue dispenser.

Anna:
: clerk and record keeper. She receives reports only when reachable, available, and procedurally able. Her records contain claims and provenance, not truth.

Elias:
: guard or enforcement actor. He acts from institutional knowledge, role duty, thresholds, suspicion, and availability. He may question or watch the wrong person.

Reeve:
: local authority. The reeve has limited authority, time, resources, bias, jurisdiction, and procedural power.

### Recommended supporting locals

Use several ordinary locals to create social density and plausible wrong suspicion. Keep each simple but causal.

Iva:
: creditor or household head connected to Mara's debt pressure. Iva is a motive source, not an exposition machine.

Nika:
: tavern keeper or public food-service actor. Nika provides food, gossip, visibility, and rumor distortion.

Petra:
: weaver or neighbor with routine visibility near Tomas's house.

Rafi:
: apprentice or mill helper who may have access to mill storage and be falsely suspected.

Soren:
: farmhand or carrier whose absence, route, or prior dispute can mislead suspicion.

Lena:
: household member or neighbor who can report stale or partial information.

Corin:
: guard assistant, messenger, or office runner if a second enforcement actor is needed.

Do not include Oren as traveler/adventurer/possible companion in the first proof. Move him to the second proof unless he is rewritten as an ordinary local with no companion/expedition role.

## The missing-property miracle

The proof chain must be able to occur with a human or without one.

### 1. Expectation exists

Tomas expects `coin_stack_01` or a comparable property item to be in `strongbox_tomas`.

The expectation must have provenance:

```text
Tomas checked the strongbox yesterday
or
Tomas placed the coins there
or
Tomas recorded the storage in a private note
```

An empty container is not evidence to someone with no expectation.

### 2. Opportunity and motive exist

Mara may believe Tomas has coins because of gossip, past work, overheard speech, visible routine, debt pressure, or previous observation. The belief may be uncertain.

Motive is not destiny. The fixture creates possibility, not an outcome script.

Mara may:

- steal the coins;
- search and fail;
- abandon attempt;
- move property without intending theft;
- be interrupted;
- be observed;
- choose not to violate norms;
- hide or spend the property later;
- confess, lie, or continue ordinary life.

### 3. Action passes the pipeline

The taking or moving action must check:

- physical reach;
- door/container access;
- item presence;
- carrying capacity;
- actor knowledge or search intent;
- willingness to violate property/privacy norms;
- time cost;
- noise/visibility;
- interruption windows;
- reservations;
- trace generation;
- observation hooks;
- event commit.

No content file may directly change inventory as the normal proof path.

### 4. Traces may exist

Possible traces:

- disturbed container;
- open/closed state anomaly;
- missing item as absence marker;
- faint sound;
- partial glimpse;
- moved object;
- lock scratch;
- footprint or mud only if causally supported;
- social trace such as nervous behavior or rumor;
- erasure trace if someone cleans, repairs, hides, or lies.

Traces should usually be ambiguous. A trace is not a clue created for the player.

### 5. Discovery occurs through contradiction

Tomas discovers absence by checking/searching the strongbox or being told by someone who checked.

Discovery must create:

- observation of contents;
- contradiction link to expectation;
- belief update;
- possible emotional response;
- candidate goals such as search, ask household, report, conceal, accuse, wait.

The system must preserve wrong alternatives:

```text
coins moved by permitted actor
Tomas misremembered
coins paid out earlier
Elena borrowed them
Mara took them
Rafi had access
container search was incomplete
```

### 6. Witness information is partial

Elena may have a low-confidence sound belief:

```text
heard brief metal/wood sound near Tomas's room
confidence low
possible causes: house settling, Tomas, Elena misheard, someone moving, animal, container
```

She must not know "Mara stole the coins" unless she actually observed enough to justify it, and even then interpretation may be uncertain.

### 7. Speech propagates claims

Tomas may ask Elena. Elena may answer with uncertainty. Tomas may report to Anna. Anna may tell Elias. Gossip may mutate.

Speech acts preserve:

- speaker;
- listeners;
- propositions;
- source beliefs;
- asserted confidence;
- lie/speculation/report kind;
- validation status;
- listener interpretation.

### 8. Record is created from claims

Anna may create an incident ledger entry if:

- reporter reaches her or valid exception applies;
- Anna is available or later receives report;
- report speech act commits;
- procedure allows entry;
- she has time/materials;
- claims are structured.

The record contains claims and provenance. It does not learn ground truth.

### 9. Wrong suspicion arises for reasons

Wrong suspicion may attach to Rafi, Soren, Elena, Tomas himself, an outsider, or another plausible actor.

Causes may include:

- access opportunity;
- presence near house;
- prior dispute;
- debt rumor;
- biased interpretation;
- uncertain testimony losing uncertainty as it travels;
- trace misread;
- social prejudice;
- record wording;
- absence during questioning;
- poor evidence threshold.

The correct culprit is not required. The point is legible fallibility.

### 10. Possession parity proves no sacred player

A player can act as Mara, switch to Tomas, and fail to use Mara-only knowledge as Tomas.

Required behavior:

- Tomas's embodied view lacks hidden truth;
- Tomas can search because he expects property;
- Tomas cannot truthfully accuse Mara without actor-known basis;
- Tomas may make reckless accusation if that act is modeled as speculation/accusation with risk;
- previous body remains ordinary agent with needs, intentions, culpability, and relationships;
- debug can show human possession history as non-diegetic metadata.

## Required scenarios

### strongbox_001

Mara steals or moves coins from Tomas's strongbox. Tomas does not know until checking or being told. Elena has a low-confidence sound belief or equivalent uncertain observation. Event log explains the chain.

### expectation_contradiction_001

Tomas expects property in a container. The property is absent. Search/check creates observation and contradiction. Candidate goals arise from modeled belief/motive, not script.

### possession_parity_001

Player acts as Mara. Player switches to Tomas or Elias. New body lacks player-only knowledge. Previous body remains ordinary actor with needs, intentions, risk, relationships, and culpability.

### report_record_001

Actor reports missing property to Anna. Report is a speech act. Clerk receives it only if available/reachable or explicit exception applies. Incident record is created from claims and provenance. Institution does not learn ground truth.

### wrong_suspicion_001

Partial testimony, rumor, absence, bias, poor inference, or trace interpretation creates suspicion toward the wrong actor. Wrong suspicion has visible causes. Correct culprit is not required.

### no_human_day_001

Village runs without human input. Agents sleep, eat, work, rest, speak minimally, and respond to modeled interruptions. No event references player identity. Debug can attach afterward and inspect state.

### view_filtering_001

Embodied view model hides ground truth. Debug view model reveals truth. Same event history supports both.

### replay_rebuild_001

Event log rebuilds state and relevant projections. Replay catches mismatched data version or migration issue. Major causes remain inspectable.

## Definition of done

The first proof is done when a player can:

1. enter the village in TUI embodied mode;
2. possess major actors in debug mode;
3. move through rooms and doors;
4. inspect/use containers and possessions;
5. sleep, eat, work, wait, talk minimally, and follow/interrupt routines;
6. steal, move, hide, search for, or discover missing property;
7. see discovery through expectation contradiction;
8. observe partial or ambiguous trace information;
9. report a missing item to a clerk or authority;
10. watch a report become a record;
11. watch wrong suspicion arise for legible reasons;
12. switch bodies without transferring actor knowledge;
13. verify no player-only verbs exist;
14. inspect causal chains in debug mode;
15. replay major consequences;
16. run the village without human input;
17. explain who knows what, why, from what source, with what confidence, and how they may be wrong.

## Failure conditions

The first proof fails if:

- ordinary life is shallow;
- the TUI cannot play or inspect it;
- the player has special verbs;
- institutions learn ground truth;
- debug knowledge leaks into embodied play;
- theft/report logic works only through scripts;
- beliefs lack source;
- records lack author/provenance;
- replay cannot rebuild major consequences;
- the proof needs an LLM;
- the proof needs a road threat, bounty, companion, or expedition to feel meaningful.

## Explicit non-goals

Not first proof:

- road threat;
- bounty or proof/payment;
- companion recruitment;
- stale-lead travel;
- old quarry or threat site;
- detailed combat;
- large region;
- procedural town generation;
- freeform LLM dialogue;
- graphical client;
- adventure class fantasy;
- objective quest tracker.

Ordinary life first. Adventure later.
