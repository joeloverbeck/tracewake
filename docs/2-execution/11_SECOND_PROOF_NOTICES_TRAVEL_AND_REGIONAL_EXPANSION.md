# Second Proof: Notices, Travel, and Regional Expansion

## Purpose

The second proof preserves the deferred direction without hijacking the first proof.

It begins only after The Missing Property Village passes. It extends proven first-proof machinery into public artifacts, route risk, stale leads, companion recruitment, travel, proof/payment, and simple regional expansion.

The second proof is still questless. It is not a bounty quest system. It is not an expedition script. It is not a drama director.

## Why it is not first

Road-threat/travel is valuable, but it depends on first-proof capabilities:

- ordinary routines;
- property/custody;
- source-backed beliefs;
- actor-known view models;
- report speech acts;
- institutional records;
- fallible procedure;
- wrong suspicion;
- no-human simulation;
- event sourcing and replay;
- debug causal inspection;
- no player privilege.

Without those, notices become quests, roads become scene transitions, companions become party members, proof becomes completion state, and payment becomes reward spawn.

The first proof must explain a missing coin before the second proof can explain a stale road notice.

## Entry requirements

Do not start second proof until all are true:

- Phase 4 exit checklist passes;
- first proof definition of done passes;
- no-human first-proof run passes;
- first-proof golden scenarios are automated;
- replay rebuilds first-proof event/belief/record projections;
- TUI can play and inspect first-proof features;
- actor-known and debug view separation is regression-tested;
- institution non-omniscience is proven;
- no player-only verbs exist;
- data validation rejects quest/player/outcome fields;
- team accepts that second-proof features cannot weaken first-proof invariants.

## Second proof identity

Working name: **The Stale Notice Road**.

The proof chain:

```text
a route threat or hazard affects someone
 -> survivor, witness, guard, traveler, or record source reports it
 -> local authority evaluates partial claims
 -> notice may be authored, authorized, posted, ignored, destroyed, forged, or left stale
 -> actors read the notice and form source-bound beliefs/leads
 -> an actor may seek companions, negotiate, travel, delay, refuse, or ignore
 -> route/site truth may diverge from public belief
 -> proof/payment, if any, depends on institutional procedure and evidence
 -> debug explains truth, public claims, stale information, route events, and outcomes
```

At no point is there an objective quest target.

## Scope

### Notices

A notice is a physical/public artifact with structured claims.

```yaml
Notice:
  id: notice_north_road_threat_001
  issuer: reeves_office
  author: actor_anna
  authorized_by: actor_reeve
  physical_artifact: notice_sheet_001
  posted_at: notice_board_square
  structured_claims:
    - proposition: ThreatReportedOnRoute(north_road)
      source_record: ledger_entry_road_threat_001
      confidence: 0.61
    - proposition: LastReportedNear(old_bridge)
      source_record: ledger_entry_road_threat_001
      confidence: 0.44
  last_verified: 142-08-12T10:00
  status: posted
```

Notice lifecycle:

```text
drafted -> authorized -> written -> carried -> posted -> read
 -> copied/ignored/acted_on/updated/stale/removed/destroyed/forged/contradicted
```

Reading a notice creates actor-known belief/lead. It does not create truth.

### Public artifacts

Public artifacts may include:

- notice board;
- ledger excerpt;
- posted warning;
- contract/request sheet;
- public payment terms;
- copied notice;
- altered/defaced notice.

Each artifact needs author/issuer/source/provenance/physical state.

### Route threat

A route threat may be:

- bandit group abstracted as process;
- dangerous animal;
- flooded bridge;
- sickness/disease marker later;
- landslide/treefall;
- social hazard such as patrol/extortion;
- rumor of threat with no current threat.

Threats are processes with events/traces, not combat encounters spawned for the player.

Route threat may produce:

- injury/death if modeled;
- survivor report;
- missing traveler;
- physical trace;
- fear belief;
- route avoidance;
- supply delay;
- institutional response;
- stale public belief.

Detailed combat remains deferred unless ordinary action/event/trace/replay contracts can support it.

### Stale leads

Stale information is a feature.

A lead may be stale because:

- threat moved;
- witness was wrong;
- route was cleared;
- notice not updated;
- claimant lied;
- event was rumor;
- old evidence decayed;
- someone else resolved the situation;
- institution lacks verification.

The TUI must label actor-known staleness only when the actor has basis. Debug may show actual staleness.

### Companion recruitment

Recruitment is a speech/social/contract flow, not party UI.

A companion has:

- needs;
- fear;
- trust;
- payment expectation;
- schedule conflicts;
- relationships;
- risk tolerance;
- ability;
- knowledge;
- refusal conditions;
- autonomy.

Recruitment checks:

- actor knows candidate;
- candidate reachable;
- candidate willing to listen;
- actor has source-bound lead/request;
- payment/share/obligation if any;
- candidate evaluates risk and trust;
- candidate can refuse, delay, lie, demand proof, abandon, or betray if modeled.

No companion waits because the player accepted a quest.

### Travel

Travel is an event chain:

```text
form destination intention
 -> choose route from belief
 -> prepare if needed
 -> depart
 -> traverse segment
 -> consume time/fatigue/safety
 -> observe/miss traces/events
 -> interruption/risk event may occur
 -> arrive, divert, turn back, rest, become lost, or fail
```

Travel cannot teleport actors to a truth marker.

### Route risk

Route risk uses:

- actual process state;
- public/institutional beliefs;
- actor beliefs;
- weather/time if modeled;
- company/companions;
- fatigue/safety;
- visibility;
- prior reports;
- random draws with audit if meaningful.

Risk does not scale because player is bored or because a lead was accepted.

### Proof/payment

Payment or proof belongs only after records/funds/custody work.

A payment offer requires:

- issuer;
- authority;
- available funds/custody;
- terms;
- proof required;
- record;
- acceptance/refusal;
- dispute process.

Proof may be:

- physical item;
- witness testimony;
- trace;
- confession;
- returned property;
- record;
- corpse/body/injury evidence if modeled;
- route condition inspection.

Proof is interpreted by institutions. It is not completion.

Possible outcomes:

- accepted;
- refused;
- partial;
- delayed;
- disputed;
- fraudulent;
- insufficient;
- paid by wrong source;
- stale closure;
- no resolution.

### Optional observer story sifting

Allowed:

- group route-threat chain after events;
- summarize no-human regional simulation;
- highlight stale notice contradictions;
- surface unresolved incidents;
- debug wrong public beliefs.

Forbidden:

- spawn threat;
- choose where clue appears;
- pace danger;
- select culprit;
- force companion loyalty;
- create proof;
- reveal hidden truth in embodied mode.

## TUI gates

Second proof TUI must allow actor-filtered play:

- read physical notice;
- inspect claims/source/staleness as actor knows them;
- ask/tell/report about notice;
- recruit or fail to recruit companion;
- prepare/travel/wait/rest;
- inspect route traces actor can perceive;
- submit proof/report to authority;
- read accessible records;
- debug inspect truth vs public belief.

The lead notebook must show claims, not objectives.

Bad:

```text
Quest: Clear North Road
Objective: Kill bandits at true camp
Reward: 100 coins
```

Good:

```text
Lead: Reported danger on north road
Source: notice posted by reeve's office
Known claims: traveler reported attack near old bridge two days ago
Uncertainty: report may be stale; no recent verification known
Possible actions: ask clerk, speak to traveler, avoid road, travel with company
```

## No-human gate

Second proof must run without human input.

Required no-human possibilities:

- report creates notice;
- actors read notice;
- actors ignore, avoid, gossip, or act on notice;
- route truth changes independently;
- notice becomes stale;
- another actor resolves, worsens, or misreports situation;
- institution delays update;
- no protagonist gravity.

## Replay/debug gates

Debug must answer:

- who reported the route threat?
- what did the notice claim?
- who authorized it?
- who read it?
- who believed it?
- why was it stale?
- what was true on the route?
- why did companion accept/refuse?
- what route events occurred?
- why was proof accepted/refused?
- which first-proof systems were reused?

Replay must rebuild notices, reads, leads, route events, companion speech, proof records, and payment/custody if present.

## Tests

Required golden scenarios:

- `notice_lifecycle_001`;
- `stale_notice_001`;
- `read_notice_actor_known_001`;
- `route_travel_001`;
- `companion_refusal_001`;
- `route_truth_public_belief_diverge_001`;
- `proof_refused_001`;
- `payment_custody_001`;
- `regional_boundary_event_001`.

Property tests:

- no notice lacks author/issuer/source claims/posting event;
- notice reading creates belief only for readers/listeners;
- stale notice does not auto-update to truth;
- route travel consumes time and events;
- companion is ordinary actor with needs/beliefs;
- payment requires funds/custody/procedure;
- no quest state drives truth.

## Forbidden shortcuts

- objective quest board;
- notice auto-updates from truth;
- route marker points to real threat;
- threat spawns when player accepts lead;
- companion becomes player inventory;
- payment appears from nowhere;
- proof interpreted by completion flag;
- combat encounter forced on arrival;
- NPCs wait for player;
- story sifter changes events;
- first-proof regressions accepted.

## Exit criteria

The second proof exits when:

- notice lifecycle works as physical, source-bound artifact;
- actors form leads by reading/hearing, not by quest assignment;
- route travel is eventful and replayable;
- public belief and route truth can diverge;
- companion recruitment is ordinary social action;
- proof/payment uses institution procedure and custody;
- no-human route/public-artifact simulation runs;
- debug explains stale information and consequences;
- no player privilege, quest ontology, or director logic appears.
