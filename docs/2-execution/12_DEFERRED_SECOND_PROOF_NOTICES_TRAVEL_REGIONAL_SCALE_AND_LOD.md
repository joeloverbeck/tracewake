## Lock rule

Second-proof work is blocked until:

- first-proof certification passes;
- Phase 4 institution/record gates pass where notices, reports, proof, payments, or authority are needed;
- LOD architecture gates have a certification plan;
- no-human ordinary life remains certified after any added travel or regional pressure;
- replay/projection/random-source gates cover regional inputs.

A future spec that introduces notice boards, travel threats, regional incidents, LOD promotion, or story sifting before these gates pass is invalid unless it is a remediation of a named gate failure.

## Notice doctrine

A notice is a world artifact carrying claims. It is not a quest.

A notice may be stale, forged, underfunded, ignored, removed, contradicted, resolved by someone else, misunderstood, or procedurally invalid. It does not imply target truth, target location, reward availability, player ownership, or automatic completion recognition.

## Travel doctrine

Travel is ordinary world action. It requires routes, risk, time, resources, knowledge, access, events, reservations, interruptions, and replay.

Travel may not spawn danger because the human needs stakes. Route threats arise from modeled causes, boundary inputs, traces, reports, or regional processes.

## LOD doctrine

LOD is replay-visible ontology, not a performance-only collapse. Promotion and demotion require events and ancestry. A promoted actor or institution receives modeled knowledge, records, memories, and summary ancestry, not filled-in truth.

Future LOD and time-acceleration proof must declare temporal ancestry,
interval, cadence, resolution, fidelity, event ancestry, information ancestry,
and known-to-whom status for every summary or promoted state. Any accelerated
or regional divergence from full-resolution processing must be declared,
bounded, and tested against fairness and epistemic constraints.

Promotion and demotion proof must show that actors, institutions, leads, and
views gain no omniscient temporal facts during LOD transitions. Future
time-accelerated or regional processes must include skipped or deferred
cognition accounting so acceleration cannot invisibly starve actor classes or
silently script outcomes. Deferred-scale fairness declarations cross-reference
the consolidated diagnostics home in
`10_TESTING_OBSERVABILITY_DIAGNOSTICS_AND_REVIEW_ARTIFACTS.md`; this document
does not define fairness formulas, windows, thresholds, regional cadence
values, LOD tolerances, or promotion algorithms.

## Story-sifting doctrine

Story sifting is observer/projection work after events occur. It may cluster, summarize, highlight, and explain. It may not cause events, repair pacing, select culprits, spawn clues, or expose hidden truth in embodied mode.

## LLM boundary

LLMs, when introduced, are language and speech-act surfaces only. They may render, paraphrase, summarize, or parse candidate speech acts behind validation. They may not author facts, plan agents, decide truth, create hidden evidence, grant knowledge, mutate state, or repair plots.

Core gameplay must operate with LLMs disabled.

## Second-proof entry gate `SECOND-PROOF-ENTRY`

This gate passes only when:

1. First proof and Phase 4 gates pass where relevant.
2. Notices are records/claims, not quest entries.
3. Travel uses ordinary action pipeline and replayable duration/reservation semantics.
4. Regional inputs declare source, cadence, scope, delivery event, affected claims, and replay visibility.
5. LOD promotion/demotion events preserve ancestry and holder-known limits.
6. Story-sifting projections are read-only observer artifacts.
7. LLM-disabled tests pass.
8. Negative fixtures reject objective markers, reward-on-completion flags, route-threat spawns, and story-director injections.

## Reason this remains in execution

The second proof remains in `docs/2-execution/` because it defines gates future execution must respect. It is not moved to reference because premature implementation is a known project risk. Keeping it here as a locked contract makes the barrier explicit.
