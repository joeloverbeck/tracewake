# First Playable Scope and Acceptance Gates

## Core claim

The first serious implementation target is a small, inspectable, TUI-playable ordinary-life village whose people can be wrong for the right reasons.

The canonical first miracle is missing property/theft. It is not the product identity. It is the first proof that physical state, subjective belief, memory, traces, speech, records, households, institutions, TUI view models, possession, and replay are one causal system.

## First domain

The first domain is neutral medieval-ish ordinary life without magic.

Use it because it makes ordinary causality legible:

- homes;
- rooms;
- doors;
- roads;
- containers;
- storage;
- food;
- work;
- wages/payment;
- households;
- local authority;
- notice board;
- slow information;
- testimony;
- gossip;
- public records;
- household privacy;
- property expectations;
- simple money/custody;
- local travel.

The kernel must not become medieval, fantasy, feudal, religious, combat-centric, monster-aware, or magic-aware. Domain flavor is content. Causal contracts are kernel doctrine.

## Scale

Target:

```text
10-30 high-detail agents
or at most a few dozen
```

A stricter early proof may start around 10-20 named high-detail actors. Optional low-LOD background people are allowed only if they are causally honest, identity-bearing, and promotable. They may not be crowd props.

## Required ordinary substrate

The first village must establish enough ordinary life to make disruption meaningful.

Prioritize:

- homes and rooms;
- doors and access;
- containers and storage;
- food and hunger;
- sleep and fatigue;
- work and wages/payment;
- money custody;
- ownership/access/control/proof/belief distinctions;
- households;
- basic social interaction;
- perception;
- memory/belief;
- typed claims;
- traces;
- search;
- structured speech acts;
- reports;
- records;
- local authority;
- TUI embodied play;
- debug possession;
- deterministic replay.

Do not prioritize:

- detailed combat;
- monsters;
- magic;
- large world maps;
- authored mysteries;
- procedural quest boards;
- LLM freeform NPCs;
- graphics;
- expedition content;
- macroeconomics;
- companion systems;
- regional conquest;
- detailed disease/demography.

Adventure may emerge later from ordinary systems. It must not be used to avoid building them.

## First playable actions

A first serious village should support staged versions of:

- look/perceive surroundings;
- move between adjacent places;
- open/close doors where possible;
- inspect containers;
- take/place/store/hide items;
- eat from available food/service;
- sleep;
- wait;
- perform work routine;
- receive/pay money;
- buy simple food/service;
- search a place/container;
- speak through structured speech acts;
- ask/tell/report/deny/lie/gossip/accuse/refuse;
- read notices/records;
- report to clerk/reeve;
- continue current intention;
- debug possess another high-detail ordinary actor;
- debug inspect causality, belief, traces, records, plans, and replay.

Each committed world-affecting action must be NPC-possible under equivalent conditions.

## First households

Households must exist early.

They provide:

- homes;
- membership;
- sleeping locations;
- food access;
- shared/private storage;
- ownership ambiguity;
- access permissions;
- privacy expectations;
- routine coordination;
- care obligations;
- domestic conflict;
- expectation contradiction;
- secrecy and protection;
- inheritance hooks for later systems.

The missing-property proof is weak without households.

## First public institution

The first public institution should be a modest clerk/reeve/town-office structure.

It should prove:

- office hours;
- report intake;
- report refusal/delay;
- records;
- limited authority;
- limited funds;
- procedure;
- bias/credibility/social position effects;
- jurisdiction;
- notice posting when in scope;
- stale records;
- wrong suspicion;
- non-omniscience.

It must not be a quest hub.

## First economy

The first economy should be small but causal.

Required:

- food objects or service abstractions under custody;
- hunger pressure;
- sleep/fatigue pressure;
- basic work routines;
- wages/payment or promises;
- money custody and ownership/access claims;
- household/shop/tavern/workplace stock/service surfaces;
- buying, stealing, storing, hiding, paying, promising payment, and refusing payment as actions;
- no fake meter refill disconnected from storage, travel, work, or access.

Full production chains, pricing, taxes, inheritance economics, debt law, and trade routes are future work.

## First memory and belief scope

Belief fallibility is foundational. Implementation may stage depth.

Early versions should prioritize:

- actor-held typed claims;
- confidence/uncertainty;
- source/channel;
- acquisition time;
- believed event time when known;
- staleness;
- contradiction links;
- direct observation versus hearsay;
- expectation contradiction;
- structured lying;
- rumor/gossip propagation;
- wrong suspicion;
- actor-knowledge-filtered TUI views.

Full forgetting, misremembering, distortion, identity confusion, and memory reconstruction may deepen later, but early structures must not block them.

## The missing-property miracle

The proof chain must be able to occur with a human or without one.

### 1. Expectation exists

An actor expects property somewhere. The expectation has provenance:

```text
actor placed item there
actor checked item there earlier
actor recorded storage
actor was told by a trusted source
household routine implies it should be there
```

An empty container is not evidence to an actor with no expectation.

### 2. Opportunity and motive exist

Another actor may take, move, hide, borrow, or steal the item because of modeled need, belief, opportunity, risk, motive, access, routine, relationship, duty, debt, fear, or obligation.

Motive is not destiny. The scenario creates possibility, not a script.

The actor may:

- steal;
- borrow while believing permission exists;
- move item to protect it;
- search and fail;
- abandon attempt;
- be interrupted;
- be observed;
- choose not to violate norms;
- hide or spend the property later;
- confess, lie, or continue ordinary life.

### 3. Action passes ordinary validation

Taking/moving checks:

- physical reach;
- route/door/container access;
- item presence;
- actor knowledge or search intent;
- willingness to violate property/privacy norms;
- carrying/custody constraints;
- time cost;
- noise/visibility;
- interruption windows;
- risk;
- trace generation;
- observation hooks;
- event commit.

No content file may directly change inventory as the normal proof path.

### 4. Traces may exist

Possible traces:

- disturbed container;
- open/closed/locked anomaly;
- missing item as absence marker;
- faint sound;
- partial glimpse;
- moved object;
- scratch or mud only if causally supported;
- social trace such as nervous behavior or rumor;
- erasure trace if someone cleans, repairs, hides, lies, or intimidates.

Traces should often be ambiguous. A trace is not a clue created for the player.

### 5. Discovery occurs through contradiction

The victim discovers absence by checking/searching or being told by someone who did.

Discovery creates:

- observation/search event;
- contradiction link to expectation;
- typed claim/belief update;
- possible emotional response;
- candidate intentions such as search, ask, report, conceal, accuse, wait, or verify;
- alternative explanations.

Possible alternatives must survive:

```text
item moved by permitted actor
victim misremembered
item spent/paid earlier
household member borrowed it
culprit took it
another plausible actor had access
search was incomplete
```

### 6. Witness information is partial

A witness may have a low-confidence sound, glimpse, absence, or timing claim. They must not become a perfect clue dispenser.

Example:

```text
heard brief metal/wood sound near Tomas's room
confidence low
possible causes: house settling, Tomas, someone moving, animal, container
```

### 7. Speech propagates claims

Actors may ask, answer, report, gossip, deny, lie, speculate, or accuse. Speech acts preserve speaker, listener, propositions, source beliefs, asserted confidence, sincerity/speculation/lie mode, validation status, listener interpretation, and memory effects.

### 8. Record is created from claims

A clerk or local authority may create a record if procedure, access, time, materials, role, and credibility allow.

The record contains typed claims and provenance. It does not learn ground truth.

### 9. Wrong suspicion arises for reasons

Wrong suspicion may attach to any plausible actor. Causes may include access opportunity, presence near house, prior dispute, debt rumor, biased inference, uncertain testimony losing uncertainty, trace misread, social prejudice, record wording, absence during questioning, or weak evidence thresholds.

The correct culprit is not required. Legible fallibility is required.

### 10. Possession parity proves no sacred player

A player can act as one body, switch to another, and fail to use first-body knowledge as second-body truth.

Required:

- embodied view lacks hidden truth;
- search is possible from actor expectation;
- truthful accusation requires actor-known basis;
- reckless accusation/speculation/lie may be available with risk;
- previous body continues as ordinary actor;
- debug shows mismatch without leaking into embodied mode.

## Required acceptance gates

Every runnable phase must include gates for:

- no-human simulation;
- TUI/view-model playability;
- actor-knowledge filtering;
- possession parity where relevant;
- no player privilege;
- no ground-truth leakage;
- deterministic replay;
- causal ancestry inspection;
- event and trace creation;
- belief provenance;
- institutional fallibility where relevant;
- no-script compliance;
- LLM-disabled operation;
- automated regression tests.

## No-human simulation gate

Every runnable phase must run with no human controller bound.

The first serious village must run for days without human input and show, within phase scope:

- needs and routines proceeding;
- sleep and hunger mattering;
- work happening or failing;
- travel consuming time;
- property remaining in custody unless moved;
- containers and access rules mattering;
- speech acts propagating information;
- beliefs changing only through modeled channels;
- reports/records being created or refused where in scope;
- stale information remaining stale;
- events/traces/failures logged;
- no system waiting for the player.

## TUI/view-model gate

Automated TUI or view-model tests must prove:

- mechanic is reachable through embodied play;
- embodied views are actor-filtered;
- hidden truth is absent in embodied mode;
- why-not explanations work;
- committed actions pass through ordinary validation;
- debug mode can reveal causal truth;
- the TUI does not implement simulation rules;
- the TUI does not create quest state.

## Actor-knowledge leakage gate

Tests should include:

- actor cannot accuse from human-only knowledge learned through possession;
- actor cannot report culprit without modeled observation, testimony, inference, or record;
- actor cannot navigate to hidden evidence via objective marker;
- actor notebook contains only source-bound actor-known leads;
- human/debug notes cannot satisfy action preconditions;
- debug truth is visibly non-diegetic;
- institution does not recognize completion without evidence/procedure;
- stale records do not auto-correct when inspected by the human.

Leakage failures are high-severity defects.

## Replay and event gate

Tests must verify:

- meaningful actions emit events;
- meaningful failures emit events;
- absence becomes evidence only through expectation/search/perception/instruction;
- trace creation and trace removal are eventful;
- beliefs and records have provenance;
- same seed and inputs produce same meaningful event sequence;
- random draws affecting outcomes are logged or reproducible;
- snapshots/projections preserve significant ancestry;
- debug can inspect causal chains.

## Institutional fallibility gate

Institution features must include relevant failures:

- report refused;
- report accepted with partial information;
- record created with uncertain claim;
- record becomes stale;
- clerk absent or office closed;
- funds insufficient;
- payment delayed/refused;
- wrong suspicion arises from legible causes;
- jurisdiction blocks action;
- bias/credibility affects procedure;
- proof requirement not satisfied;
- record lost, misfiled, damaged, forged, or contradicted.

A perfect always-correct authority is an anti-pattern.

## No-script gate

Reject features that contain:

- player-conditioned event injection;
- quest state as primary ontology;
- guaranteed target existence;
- guaranteed reward payment;
- world waiting for player;
- automatic completion recognition;
- objective markers to ground truth;
- drama pacing mutation;
- LLM fact creation;
- hidden scripted red herrings or reveals.

Scenario seeds and HTN procedures are acceptable only when they define causal possibility, failure, interruption, and NPC use.

## LLM-disabled gate

Core mechanics must work with LLMs disabled.

Tests must prove:

- structured speech acts work without LLMs;
- records/notices render without LLMs;
- agents plan without LLMs;
- facts are not created by prose;
- LLM parse/render failure cannot corrupt state;
- disabling LLMs does not remove core gameplay.

## Canonical regression seeds

Maintain deterministic regression seeds such as:

```text
ordinary_day_no_human
container_and_access
household_storage
missing_property_discovery
uncertain_witness
lie_and_rumor
partial_report_to_clerk
wrong_suspicion
stale_notice
debug_possession_no_leak
replay_same_seed
llm_disabled_language_surface
```

These are acceptance scenarios, not task tickets.

## Explicit non-goals for first serious proof

Not first proof:

- road threat;
- bounty/proof/payment loop;
- companion recruitment;
- stale-lead travel;
- old quarry or threat site;
- detailed combat;
- large region;
- procedural town generation;
- freeform LLM dialogue;
- graphical client;
- adventure class fantasy;
- objective quest tracker.

Ordinary life first. Adventure later.

## Definition of done

The first proof is done when a player can:

1. enter the village in TUI embodied mode;
2. possess major actors in debug mode;
3. move through rooms and doors;
4. inspect/use containers and possessions;
5. sleep, eat, work, wait, talk minimally, and follow/interrupt routines;
6. steal, move, hide, search for, or discover missing property;
7. see discovery through expectation contradiction;
8. observe partial or ambiguous trace information;
9. report a missing item to a clerk/authority;
10. watch a report become a record or fail to do so;
11. watch wrong suspicion arise for legible reasons;
12. switch bodies without transferring actor knowledge;
13. verify no player-only verbs exist;
14. inspect causal chains in debug mode;
15. replay major consequences;
16. run the village without human input;
17. explain who knows what, why, from what source, with what confidence, and how they may be wrong.

Anything less is a prototype note, not foundation-compatible completion.

## 2026 hardening: acceptance gates for hidden-truth cognition

The first playable scope must now reject any phase claim that cannot prove actor-known cognition.

Additional gates:

### Truth firewall gate

For every salient autonomous or possessed action, acceptance evidence must show:

- the actor-known context used for proposal generation;
- provenance for action-relevant cognition inputs;
- no debug-only or validator-only truth in planning context;
- validator truth used only at validation/resolution;
- actor-visible feedback separated from debug-only feedback;
- replay agreement for decision traces and outcomes.

### Actor decision transaction gate

No-human ordinary life must pass through the canonical actor decision transaction or equivalent architecture. A direct scheduler shortcut is a failure even if it produces plausible event logs.

Forbidden pass conditions:

```text
hunger -> directly choose true food source
routine label -> directly emit move/work/sleep action
workplace table -> directly choose true workplace without actor-known assignment provenance
empty epistemic projection -> claim actor-known safety
string diagnostic -> treated as typed proof
continue_routine -> counted as progress without ordinary action ancestry
```

### Resourcefulness gate

A serious ordinary-life proof must include at least some modeled failure and recovery:

- unavailable food, blocked routine, closed office, occupied bed, missing item, rejected work, failed route, or similar blocker;
- actor-visible failure or uncertainty;
- replan, wait, ask, search, abandon, seek help, or produce stuck diagnostic;
- debug explanation of why the actor did not use hidden truth.

The minimum first playable does not need the full monster/caravan/notice/regional chain. It does need the machinery that would eventually allow such a chain to happen without scripts.

### Institution and record gate

Any report, notice, bounty, contract, case, payment, sanction, or work assignment must have modeled source and procedure. Acceptance must reject institutional omniscience.

### LLM-disabled gate

LLM-disabled operation remains normal. Any future LLM speech surface must validate structured speech acts and must not be necessary for deterministic acceptance.
