# Glossary

## Actor

An entity capable of perception, belief, intention, and action.

## Agent

The decision-making system associated with an actor.

## Belief

A subjective proposition held by an agent, with confidence and provenance. It may be true, false, stale, contradictory, or speculative.

## Causal graph

A graph linking events to causes, enabling conditions, intentions, observations, traces, and consequences.

## Contract

A formal obligation between parties, usually with verification, proof, reward, and sanctions.

## Domain pack

Scenario-specific content layered over the neutral core: genre, institutions, bodies, items, magic, monsters, norms, occupations, and traces.

## Embodied mode

Normal play mode in which the player sees what the controlled actor can access, plus organized actor-known information.

## Event

An immutable record of a meaningful occurrence, failed attempt, observation, report, communication, or state change.

## Event sourcing

An architecture where state changes are stored as events and current state is derived from replay/projection.

## Exogenous boundary event

An event entering the simulated region from outside, such as immigration, caravan arrival, disease, weather front, or political order.

## Ground truth

The authoritative world state and event history. Agents do not automatically know it.

## Institution

A social system with roles, records, norms, procedures, resources, and jurisdiction.

## Intention

A committed goal or plan an agent is currently pursuing.

## Lead

A player-facing clue, artifact, rumor, report, notice, or opportunity. Unlike a quest, it does not imply truth, solvability, intended player involvement, or guaranteed reward.

## LOD

Simulation level of detail. Low LOD summarizes routine processes; high LOD simulates detailed action/perception/planning.

## Norm

A social or institutional rule: obligation, prohibition, permission, status rule, procedural rule, or constitutive rule.

## Notice

A physical or public information artifact communicating structured claims.

## Possession

The player’s controller binding to an actor. Default possession is non-diegetic and does not alter truth or actor knowledge.

## Project

A long-term goal or concern that persists across interruptions.

## Projection

A derived view of event history or state, such as inventory, belief store, ledger, UI card, or case file.

## Quest

A conventional game concept avoided as a primary engine type. Quest-like experiences emerge from incidents, requests, contracts, obligations, notices, and opportunities.

## Routine

A recurring intention pattern, such as workday or mealtime, that remains conditional and interruptible.

## Salience

An observer-layer measure that helps surface interesting chains. Salience may highlight but must not cause events.

## Speech act

A typed communication action such as report, accuse, lie, ask, promise, gossip, threaten, recruit, or confess.

## Stale information

Information that may once have been accurate but no longer matches ground truth.

## Trace

A world artifact or mental/social/institutional residue left by an event.

## Violation

An action that breaks a norm. It can occur without detection or sanction.

## Why-not UI

An interface explanation of why an action is unavailable or risky in terms of actor knowledge, state, norms, resources, or preconditions.
